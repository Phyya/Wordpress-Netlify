What Kind Of Moisturizer Should I Use After A Chemical Peel

After a chemical peel, it’s crucial to use a good moisturizer to keep your skin from drying out and flaking.

Chemical peels exfoliate the skin, removing the dead outer layer along with it. Because of this, your skin is more susceptible to drying out, therefore it’s crucial that you choose a good moisturizer that will help you rehydrate while also shielding your new skin from the environment.

Choosing the right moisturizer following a chemical peel is easy if you follow these steps:

Try to find something that doesn’t clog pores. As a result, it won’t lead to clogged pores and acne.

If you have oily skin or frequently break out in pimples, select a solution that is lightweight and oil-free. As they don’t contain any oil, the oil-free alternatives are easier on the skin and less likely to lead to breakouts.

If you don’t have sensitive skin or allergies and want a cheap option that works well with most people’s complexions, an over-the-counter product is your best bet. But, there are always exceptions to this rule, and your mileage may vary depending on the specific product and your own unique circumstances.

After undergoing a chemical peel, moisturizing becomes even more crucial to your skincare routine. Chemical peels exfoliate the skin and leave a new, healthier layer on top, which is more resilient. Because of the thicker protective layer, the top layer of skin is more prone to drying out.

Choose a moisturizer with components that assist replenish moisture in the skin for this same reason. Moisturizers come in a wide variety, and each one serves a particular function.

Examples of popular moisturizers are:

Softer than lotions, creams are ideal for dry or sensitive skin. Creams are more effective at locking in moisture than lotions because they contain more oil.

Gels are fast-absorbing moisturizers that don’t leave behind any sticky residue on the skin. Gels, like creams, contain oils, but they also contain alcohol, which keeps the skin moisturized for much longer than creams do.

After a chemical peel, your skin will be very sensitive, so pick a light moisturizer. Chicago dermatologist Dr. Kavita Mariwalla, MD, advises patients to use a product that will nourish the skin without making it feel weighed down.

To help you choose the best moisturizer to use after a chemical peel, consider the following:

Choose the oil-free option. Dr. Mariwalla says that because the product is oil-free, it will not cause clogged pores. Chemical peels “may cause pores to widen and become more noticeable.”

Try to find products that contain hyaluronic acid or glycerin. Both aid in keeping skin moist and prevent dryness, which is especially useful if you have dry skin or live in a dry region.

In the days following your treatment, avoid using anything too heavy or thick (like Vaseline), since this can irritate the skin and slow down the healing process.
