What Happens During a Mild Chemical Peel?

Under local anesthetic, a gentle chemical peel is typically performed as an outpatient procedure. If you are highly anxious about having a cosmetic operation done or if you need substantial facial surgery that necessitates heavy sedation, you might be given intravenous sedation or general anesthesia. You will be required to remove all makeup and ensure that your skin is dry and clean prior to your consultation. Depending on how much facial skin needs to be treated, light chemical peels might take anywhere from 15 minutes and an hour.

Before starting treatment, your doctor will apply a thin layer of cream or gel to your face and wait for it to completely dry. Then, the physician uses cotton swabs or sponges to apply different concentrations of trichloroacetic acid solution or glycolic acid solution to your face.
