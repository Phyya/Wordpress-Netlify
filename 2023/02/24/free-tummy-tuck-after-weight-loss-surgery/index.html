TUMMY TUCK PROCEDURE

The tummy tuck procedure is performed under anesthetic. In the procedure. Dr. Brenner will make a curved incision from one hip to the other. He will then remove excess skin and fat between the bellybutton and the bikini line to create a smooth, fit look to the abdomen.

The incision will leave a scar, but Dr. Brenner will take measures to ensure the incision is no longer than it needs to be. In some cases, a circular incision can be made around the bellybutton. This surgical technique produces less visible scarring. Incision lines fade over time, and are hidden in areas that will be covered, and eventually will be almost unnoticeable.

RECOVERY

Following a tummy tuck after weight loss, you may need to stay overnight at a recovery center. This is a surgical procedure, so it may be necessary to be monitored for the first night.

You will need to limit physical activity for the first few days after your procedure, but by the end of the week you should be able to resume many normal activities. You will still need to ensure you are getting sufficient rest, so your incision can heal. The treated area will be covered with a compression garment to assist in the healing process. You will be sore after the surgery, but discomfort can be easily managed with prescription pain medication.

A tummy tuck is a surgical procedure that removes excess fat and skin from the abdomen, and tightens the underlying muscles. A tummy tuck can also be used to remove stretch marks.

The length of time needed to recover from a tummy tuck will depend on several factors, including:

Your general health. If you have medical conditions such as diabetes or high blood pressure, it will take longer for your body to heal.

Your age. Younger people heal more quickly than older adults.

The size of your incision and the extent of the surgery performed. A larger incision takes longer to heal than a small one. If other procedures such as liposuction or breast lift are performed at the same time as your tummy tuck, it will take longer for your body to recover from these procedures as well.

Your activity level before surgery and during recovery. If you are relatively inactive before surgery and take it easy during recovery, it may take less time for you to recover than if you were a very active person who resumed strenuous activity too soon after surgery
