How does the Halo Laser work?

The device emits tiny beams of light which penetrate deep into the skin without damaging it. The beam then heats up certain areas within the deeper layers of skin while leaving others alone, which causes new collagen to form around these areas while simultaneously shrinking fat cells. This combination reduces the appearance of wrinkles while filling out depressions in the skin caused by fat loss or acne scarring.

There are many lasers that can treat under eye wrinkles. The most common are the CO2 and Fractional lasers. They both work by heating up the collagen in your skin and causing it to contract. This causes a tightening effect that can reduce the appearance of wrinkles.

The CO2 laser is great for deep wrinkles and creases, but it can also be used to treat superficial ones as well. The downside is that it does take some time for results to show up (about 3-4 months). The Fractional laser works faster (about 2 months), but it doesn’t treat deep lines quite as well as the CO2 does.

Both of these treatments need multiple sessions to get optimal results. Expect anywhere from 3-6 sessions per area treated depending on how deep your wrinkles are and what kind of skin you have (dryer skin will require more sessions than oily skin).
