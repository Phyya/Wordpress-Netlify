What Is Laser Hair Removal?

Laser hair removal is the process of removing unwanted body hair with a laser beam. The laser beam targets and destroys the hair follicle without damaging the surrounding skin, preventing future growth of that particular strand of hair. The laser light penetrates the skin, targeting only those follicles that are actively growing at any given time. This makes it an effective treatment for people with dark skin tones who have trouble with other treatments like waxing or shaving.

How Does It Work?

There are two types of lasers used in this procedure: Nd:YAG lasers and Alexandrite lasers. Both types work by targeting specific pigments in the follicle that contain melanin (the pigment responsible for giving human beings their color). When these pigments are destroyed, they produce heat that damages and kills off all living tissue within its reach — including follicles (which contain pigment-producing cells).
