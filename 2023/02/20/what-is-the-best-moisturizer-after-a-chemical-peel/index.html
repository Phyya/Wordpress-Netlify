Why Utilize Serums?

There are several benefits to utilizing a serum instead of other skin care products after a face peel. Compared to other products like lotion or cream, a serum has molecules that are smaller. More active substances can be delivered straight into the layers of your skin where they are most effective since these tiny molecules can penetrate deeper into your skin than bigger ones can. This is particularly crucial if you recently underwent an exfoliating technique, such as a face peel or microdermabrasion treatment, as these procedures can cause significant damage and dryness to the stratum corneum, the outermost layer of your epidermis.

Different formulations intended to be administered topically under the skin are referred to as serums. They often have an alcohol or water basis and are made up of a combination of active chemicals, emulsifiers, and preservatives.

Retinol and vitamin C are the two active components most frequently found in serums. These components contribute to smoother skin, less fine lines and wrinkles, increased collagen formation, and higher rates of cell renewal.

Retinol functions by speeding up the cell turnover rate, which causes dead cells to shed more quickly than usual. Moreover, retinol encourages the creation of collagen, which works to both smooth out existing wrinkles and stop new ones from developing. When applied topically to the skin, vitamin C has comparable benefits; it promotes the creation of collagen while also acting as an antioxidant since it may combat free radicals in the body.
