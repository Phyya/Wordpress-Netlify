Describe the dark spots.

Dark spots are skin lesions caused by ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun. Melanin, which gives your skin its color, can be harmed and build up in the upper layers of the skin when exposed to UV rays. Hyperpigmentation or solar lentigines are the dark areas that develop from this process (LS).

Age spots, also known as liver spots, are the most prevalent kind of LS and develop on the backs of hands and other exposed regions of skin. Inflammatory or post-inflammatory pigmentation markings, such as freckles and birthmarks like blue naevi, are a less prevalent kind of LS. These marks include melasma or chloasma (the mask of pregnancy).

Sun damage can also create undesired pigmentation on other regions of your body, such as your neck, chest, and back, in addition to the dark patches that appear on your face.
