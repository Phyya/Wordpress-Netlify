There are a number of risks associated with tummy tuck surgery. These include:

Infection. Infection is the most common complication of any type of surgery and can occur if bacteria enter the wound during or after surgery.

Scarring. Tummy tuck scars may be noticeable and may not fade over time as expected. They can also become raised and thickened if they are located in areas that rub against clothing or other objects (notably, under the breasts).

Damage to nerves or blood vessels. The location of the incision may cause damage to nearby nerves or blood vessels, resulting in numbness or loss of sensation in the area below where the incision was made. This is more likely if an abdominal cut (rather than an umbilical cut) was made to remove excess skin from around your bellybutton or pelvic bones. Damage to these structures can also cause permanent scarring, which may lead to other complications such as pain or infection.

Risks of death from tummy tuck surgery include:
