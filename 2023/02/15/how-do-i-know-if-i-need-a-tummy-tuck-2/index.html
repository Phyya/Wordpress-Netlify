What they don’t tell you about tummy tucks:

Liposuction is a procedure that removes fat from areas where it is stored in the body. It does not remove skin, which is why a lot of people get lipo on their hips and buttocks only to be disappointed with how “bumpy” their skin still looks after the procedure. This is due to the fact that there are several layers of fat that run vertically through your body (called “fatty stripes”). Lipo only removes one layer of those fatty stripes at a time, which means you will have at least two layers remaining after lipo alone. These remaining layers stretch out your skin and cause it to hang loosely over your frame, making it appear saggy or loose even if you have lost some weight.

A tummy tuck removes these remaining layers by removing excess skin as well as tightening muscles underneath your belly.
