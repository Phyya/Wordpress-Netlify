Gaining Weight After A Tummy Tuck


While achieving stunning results after plastic surgery is always the goal, maintaining those results is, arguably, just as important, if not more. One of the most sought-after and transformative cosmetic procedures performed today is the tummy tuck, or abdominoplasty. More and more men and women across the country each year turn to tummy tuck to help them achieve or restore a slimmer, tighter, more appealing physique. However, when patients experience weight gain after a tummy tuck, whether due to pregnancy, lifestyle changes, or other factors, their original results may be significantly compromised.

Board-certified plastic surgeon Dr. Christopher Khorsandi and the professional team at VIP Plastic Surgery in Las Vegas, NV are dedicated to helping patients reach and maintain their ultimate aesthetic goals through the most effective and cutting-edge procedures and treatments available, including tummy tuck, as well as advising patients on the best course of action to restore their original results that may have changed over time.

WHAT DOES A TUMMY TUCK DO?

Tummy tuck is primarily an excess skin removal procedure that is designed to sculpt and contour a patient’s abdomen for a slimmer, smoother contour. Women who have had children and patients who have lost a great deal of weight often have tummy tuck surgery to remove the excess, stretched out skin that remains after weight loss. In addition, tummy tuck surgery can be an effective solution for correcting diastasis recti (separated abdominal muscles), a hernia, and other problems. In many cases, liposuction is performed as part of a tummy tuck to further enhance a patient’s results by removing stubborn deposits of fat in the abdomen.

HOW LONG DOES A TUMMY TUCK LAST?

In theory, the results of a tummy tuck can last indefinitely, depending on a patient’s lifestyle, genetics, the aging process, and other factors. Tummy tuck aftercare plays an important role in how long patients may be able to enjoy their results. In addition to following your surgeon’s postoperative guidelines for proper and efficient healing, maintaining a stable weight is essential. Tummy tuck patients should avoid significant weight fluctuations by eating a healthy, balanced diet, drinking plenty of water, and exercising regularly. It is also recommended that women be certain they are done having children before undergoing tummy tuck surgery, as weight fluctuations associated with pregnancy can also negatively impact their results.

WHAT TO DO IF YOU GAIN WEIGHT AFTER A TUMMY TUCK

In some cases, a small amount of weight gain may not have a major impact on the results of a tummy tuck. For instance, if a patient gains 5 – 10 pounds over the holiday season but subsequently loses it after a few months of returning to a normal diet and exercise routine, they may not see a difference in their figure. However, when someone gains a significant amount of weight after a tummy tuck, particularly if the weight is retained for a long period of time, the skin may once again become stretched out to the point that even losing weight will not be enough to restore their original results. In these cases, a repeat tummy tuck may be the only option for removing the loose skin and achieving an ideal figure.

It is important for patients to understand that a second or subsequent tummy tuck may be more difficult, and possibly less successful, than the first. Scar tissue, aging, and other factors may limit the surgeon’s ability to achieve results that are identical to those of the original procedure. Furthermore, patients who gain weight after their first tummy tuck still need to lose the weight and maintain a healthy, stable weight for at least six months before undergoing a second tummy tuck.

How Tummy Tuck Surgery Works

Tummy tuck surgery is a procedure that removes excess skin and fat from the abdomen and tightens the muscles of the abdominal wall. It can also be used to improve muscle tone.

During the procedure, a surgeon will make an incision along your bikini line and remove excess tissue and fat from your lower abdomen. The skin is then pulled taught and stitched back together. If your muscles are lax, they may be tightened at the same time or shortly after the surgery.

The procedure may also include liposuction, which is a surgical removal of unwanted fat deposits. Liposuction alone can be done without an abdominoplasty (tummy tuck), but most people who have liposuction also choose to get a tummy tuck because it improves their results by removing excess skin at the same time.

As with any surgery, there are risks associated with abdominoplasty including infection, bleeding, scarring and nerve damage in areas that are not numb during surgery.

A tummy tuck, also called a abdominoplasty, removes excess skin and fat from the midsection and tightens muscles of the abdominal wall. This procedure can improve muscle tone and flatten the abdomen. It may be done to treat gynecomastia (male breast enlargement), as well as for cosmetic reasons.

A tummy tuck can be performed on patients who have lost weight or those who are medically obese. This procedure is best done soon after pregnancy or major weight loss because it will help preserve muscle tone that has been lost during these times.

During a tummy tuck, several incisions will be made in the lower abdomen to remove excess skin and fat and tighten muscles that have weakened through pregnancy or other causes. The surgeon will then reshape the abdominal wall by removing excess skin and tightening any loose muscle tissue present. The surgeon will also repair any separated muscles in the abdominal wall. Some surgeons perform a liposuction technique to remove excess fat from areas besides the abdomen — including from underarms, knees or thighs — while others do not perform liposuction at all with their tummy tucks.
