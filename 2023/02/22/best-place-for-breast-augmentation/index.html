Breast augmentation is a surgical procedure in which breast implants are inserted to enlarge the size of the breasts. The implants may be placed under the skin or above the muscle (subglandular), and can be made of silicone or saline. Implant size is determined by bra cup size, and can range from nearly a full cup size to three cups.

The surgery can be performed under local anesthesia with sedation, or under general anesthesia. The incisions are usually made around the areola (the dark area surrounding the nipple) and around the inframammary fold (the crease below the breast). The recovery process takes about three weeks, but most patients are able to return to work after two weeks. During this time, patients must wear a compression garment for support.
