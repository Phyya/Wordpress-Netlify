How Long Do Teeth Whitening Last

Teeth whitening is a popular cosmetic procedure that can be performed in-office or at home. Teeth whitening treatments can last anywhere from one to three years, but the amount of time it takes for a person’s teeth to return to their original color will depend on several factors.

Factors that Affect the Length of Teeth Whitening

The duration of tooth whitening depends on several factors, including:

Age: As people age, their teeth naturally become darker and more yellow. This is due to the breakdown of the enamel and dentin layers of the tooth. Over time these natural processes break down even more and make it more difficult for whitening treatments to lighten teeth. The older you are, the longer it will take for your teeth to return to their original shade after treatment.

Health: People who have poor overall health or bad habits may have trouble maintaining the results of their whitening treatments because they are less likely to stick with a daily oral hygiene routine and visit their dentist regularly. If you fall into this category, it may take longer for your teeth to return to their original shade than someone who has good overall health and better oral hygiene habits.

Diet: Certain foods can stain your teeth over time

Many people want to know how long their teeth will stay white after they’ve completed their treatment. Here’s what you need to know:

In-office teeth whitening lasts only as long as you wear the trays every day, which is usually two weeks. At-home systems can last three months or longer if you use them twice a day every day with good oral hygiene habits (brush after every meal and floss daily).

Treatments with peroxide are most effective at lightening stains on enamel (the outer layer of your teeth). They don’t remove stains that have become embedded in dentin (the inner layer of your teeth). So if your teeth have been stained by tea, coffee or tobacco products over time, those stains may remain even after your treatment is complete.

Your lifestyle and diet

How Long Does Teeth Whitening Take At Home

There are two ways to get teeth whitening. You can either visit a dentist, or use an at-home kit. The dentist will do it in one visit, but if you want to do it at home, it’s going to take several weeks of using the kits and some patience.

Home Teeth Whitening Kits

The best way to whiten your teeth is by using a home teeth whitening kit. These come with instructions on how to use them, and they’re also safe and effective if used correctly. However, you can’t expect instant results from these kits as they take time to work properly.

At-home teeth whitening kits are available at drugstores, grocery stores and other retailers. They typically contain peroxide gel containing a very low concentration of hydrogen peroxide — around 10 percent — that’s activated by light or heat.

The active ingredient in these products is usually carbamide peroxide or hydrogen peroxide, which breaks down into oxygen and water when it comes in contact with saliva. Oxygen helps remove stains from your teeth, while saliva neutralizes the acid generated by bacteria on tooth surfaces as you brush your teeth with toothpaste or rinse with water after eating acidic foods like oranges or tomatoes to create an environment where the whitening agent can work effectively.
