Getting Tummy Tuck Before Pregnancy

The tummy tuck is a common plastic surgery procedure for women who have excess fat and skin in their abdominal area. The procedure can also help with stretch marks, which are sometimes referred to as striae gravidarum. A tummy tuck is usually performed on women who are pregnant after giving birth to correct issues such as loose skin and stretch marks, but it’s important to know that getting a tummy tuck before pregnancy can be risky.

Tummy tucks can be performed through a variety of methods, but most involve an incision that runs from side to side across the abdomen and down to the pubic bone. The surgeon removes any excess skin, fat tissue and muscle that is hanging over the pelvic area, leaving behind only smooth skin. He may also perform liposuction if necessary. The wound will be closed with sutures or staples and covered with bandages or strips of surgical tape called steri-strips.
