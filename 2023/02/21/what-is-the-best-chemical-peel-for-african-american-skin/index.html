Superficial Chemical Peel

One of the most common cosmetic procedures is a chemical peel. It is a safe and effective procedure that can be used to reduce wrinkles, hyperpigmentation, and acne scars in the skin.

The goal of superficial chemical peels is to exfoliate the outermost layers of dead skin cells in order to improve the appearance of your complexion. These procedures last for about 30 minutes and are carried out in a medical spa or doctor’s office. Up to four weeks can pass following a superficial chemical peel before additional treatments are needed.

A superficial chemical peel is a non-invasive procedure that works by liquifying the epidermis with a chemical solution. After two to three weeks, the remedy causes your skin to peel off, exposing newer, healthier skin beneath.

All skin types can benefit from a superficial chemical peel. It works best for those who have mild acne scarring and fine lines or wrinkles. A deeper chemical peel, carried out by a dermatologist, can be necessary to remove deeper scars.
