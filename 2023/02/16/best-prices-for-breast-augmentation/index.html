Breast Implant Types

There are two types of breast implants: silicone gel and saline (saltwater).

Silicone gel implants are filled with silicone gel, which resembles human tissue and is more durable than saline implants. Silicone gel implants are often used in breast reconstruction because they appear more natural. Silicone gel implants may also be preferred by women who have had breast surgery in the past and want to avoid future problems with their breasts.

Saline (saltwater) implants are filled with saltwater that can leak out over time. Saline implants are less expensive than silicone gel implants because they do not require special handling when implanted in the body. However, saline implants may cause capsular contracture, which occurs when scar tissue forms around the implant capsule and squeezes it into a smaller shape than intended by your doctor. Capsular contracture can cause pain, infection, or even rupture of the implant.
