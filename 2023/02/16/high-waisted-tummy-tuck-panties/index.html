How Can I Prepare for My Fleur-De-Lis Tummy Tuck?

If you want a flatter stomach after pregnancy, a standard tummy tuck is a terrific choice. Yet, a typical stomach tuck may not be the ideal answer for patients who have lost a lot of weight.

Fleur-de-lis abdominoplasty is one such solution. A conventional tuck is a body contouring treatment that involves the horizontal removal of extra skin. The removal of excess skin in the vertical and horizontal planes is a hallmark of the fleur-de-lis tuck. The skin is lowered and drawn in toward the midsection during this surgery. There is a marked improvement in the appearance of the abdominal muscles after they have been drawn together and shaped.

What Makes a Fleur-de-Lis Tummy Tuck Different from a Traditional One?
The fleur-de-lis stomach tuck differs from the standard abdominoplasty in a number of ways. Cutting is the most important part. The incision for this procedure, which consists of the standard hip-to-hip line and a vertical incision from the breast bone to the pubic area, takes the form of a fleur-de-lis, hence the name. This is where the two cuts, one horizontal and one vertical, come together.

The second incision is made so that more excess skin can be cut away from the abdomen’s sides. Tightening and trimming can be done above and below the incision line when a horizontal incision is made. More circumferential contouring is achieved in the abdominal area by making a vertical incision (but not around the sides to the back).

Is Fleur-de-Lis (FDL) Abdominoplasty Right for You?
Those who have lost a lot of weight and kept it off for a long time are the most likely candidates for this operation. Those who have had gastric bypass or another form of surgical weight loss can benefit greatly from this choice.
