What Is Laser Hair Removal?

Laser hair removal is a process in which the body’s hairs are removed using laser energy. If you were to look at the process under a microscope, you would see that each strand of hair has a bulbous root called the follicle. The laser energy is absorbed by this bulbous root, causing it to heat up and eventually die off.

There are two different types of lasers used for this treatment: Alexandrite and Nd: YAG (Neodymium-doped Yttrium Aluminum Garnet). Both types have been approved by the FDA for use in hair removal treatment, although Alexandrite lasers seem to be more commonly used today due to their low cost compared with Nd: YAG lasers.
