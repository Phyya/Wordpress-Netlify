What Is Abdominoplasty?

Abdominoplasty targets excess skin and fat and repairs weakened or damaged abdominal muscles to deliver a flatter, tighter, slimmer waistline. This procedure is ideal for patients with loose skin and stubborn fatty deposits after significant weight loss, pregnancy, or due to the natural process of aging. With an incision made just above the pubic line, your plastic surgeon will repair stomach muscles and remove the unwanted skin and fat creating a streamlined, natural-looking contour.

Healing From Abdominoplasty

Abdominoplasty is a major surgery and you will need some time to heal. Most patients can resume normal activities like driving, work, and moderate exercise 3-4 weeks post-op. Tenderness, swelling, and redness generally last for up to four weeks, but for some patients it can be a little longer. After four weeks, most patients report that they feel almost fully recovered, but minor swelling can linger for weeks and months to come.

Swelling After Abdominoplasty Is Normal

It is normal and expected that patients will experience swelling after a tummy tuck. This area is rich with blood vessels and lymphatic vessels (the body‚Äôs drainage system), but abdominoplasty surgery will impair the regular functions of these systems, slowing down the drainage and resulting in a fluid buildup, or swelling.

Swelling May Come And Go

The majority of swelling will resolve in the first couple of weeks after your surgery. But it is important to note that healing and recovery is a process and you will likely experience fluctuations in the amount of swelling day to day, or even throughout one day. Diet and activity can affect swelling. For instance, standing for a long period of time can cause fluids to pool and increase swelling, and drinking plenty of water and a healthy diet including plenty of nutrient-rich fruits and vegetables can speed up healing and reduce swelling.

When Will Swelling Go Away?

You will absolutely be able to see a difference in your waistline in the first few days following a tummy tuck, but you should expect to experience some pain, swelling, bruising, and redness for several weeks. The majority of bruising and swelling will resolve in 4-6 weeks, but it could take up to one full year for all swelling to disappear and for you to be able to fully appreciate the final results of your abdominoplasty.

Tips For Faster Healing And To Minimize Swelling

Swelling is unavoidable after a tummy tuck, but these tips will help you minimize swelling and speed up your recovery:

  • Get plenty of rest and elevate your legs when possible
  • Follow your surgeon’s instructions
  • Wear your compression garment
  • Stay hydrated
  • Move a bit each day (avoid rigorous activity, but gentle walking when cleared by your surgeon will promote healing)
  • Eat a nutrient-rich, low sodium diet
