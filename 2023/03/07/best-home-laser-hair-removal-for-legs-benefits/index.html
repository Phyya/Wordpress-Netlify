What Are The Side Effects Of Laser Hair Removal?

A huge benefit of laser hair removal is that the side effects are minimal. You may encounter some discomfort during the procedure while the hairs are literally being zapped, which may prickle, but it should not hurt.

After the laser hair treatment, your skin may show signs of irritation. This may be in the form of redness, swelling, itching or tingling. This is normal and should subside within a few hours, just like other hair removal methods. You may notice some slight alterations to the pigment of the skin – it may get marginally lighter or darker, but this is usually only temporary. After your laser hair removal, your skin may feel delicate to touch and will be more sensitive to light. This is easily solved by ensuring you cover it with adequate sun protection.

Some rare side effects of laser hair removal are blistering, crusting or scarring. These are more likely to happen if you over-treat your skin by going over an area more than once. If they do occur, make sure to keep them clean to prevent infection.

Overall, most people find they recover from laser hair removal very quickly with minimal side effects and no complications. The recovery period is so short that you could even have a session during your lunch break and return to work with no issues.

Does It Cause Infertility?

No, laser hair removal does not cause infertility.

If you are planning to have laser hair removal on your bikini line, you may be concerned about it affecting your fertility. Laser hair removal cannot cause infertility or affect the ovaries because it does not go deep enough through the body tissues to be able to reach them. The laser is controlled so that it only targets the hair follicle and it cannot pass through the abdominal wall which is strong and thick due to the presence of muscle.

In addition, the beam from the laser is made up of light energy. Light energy cannot break molecules apart and therefore cannot affect the DNA or cells. This is because it is not a form of ionizing radiation like x-rays are, which can pass through the body and affect cells on a molecular level.

However, if you are pregnant or trying for a baby, it is best to delay your laser hair treatment until after you have had the baby and finished breastfeeding as there has not been enough research into laser hair removal during pregnancy.
