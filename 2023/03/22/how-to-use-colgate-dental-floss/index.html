How To Floss Back Teeth

How to floss back teeth.

Flossing is one of the most important things you can do for your oral health, but it’s also one of the most difficult. It’s not that flossing is hard; it’s just that the way many people floss their teeth isn’t very effective.

Here’s how to floss correctly:

First, wrap about 18 inches of dental floss around your middle fingers (or use an interdental brush).

Next, insert the floss into your mouth between two teeth and use a gentle up-and-down motion to scrape away any plaque or food particles that might be caught between them.

Continue this process until all of your teeth (front and back) are clean of plaque and debris.
