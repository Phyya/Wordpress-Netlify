Swelling after tummy tuck surgery is normal. The amount of swelling will depend on the amount of surgery performed (the more skin and tissue removed, the greater the swelling), your age, and your overall health.

Surgeons always expect some degree of swelling after any surgical procedure, but it can be especially challenging after a tummy tuck due to the extent of liposuction performed as well as the degree of tension placed on the abdominal muscles during surgery.

It is important that patients understand that there is a difference between swelling and bruising following surgery. Swelling is normal, but bruising is not. Bruising happens when blood vessels leak into the surrounding tissue, causing discoloration and pain in the area. Bruising should clear up within two weeks after your surgery, but if you notice any unusual bruises or bleeding at any time afterwards, you should contact your doctor immediately.

If you do not wear compression garments for this time period, you may see that your swelling comes back or even gets worse due to lymphedema (an abnormal collection of lymphatic fluid).
